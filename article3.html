Sumedang Ready to create an Aerial Cable-Free City

Radioerksfm.com (Sumedang) – The Sumedang Regency Government continues to demonstrate its commitment to improving urban infrastructure by removing messy overhead cables.

This effort aims to create an orderly, aesthetically pleasing urban environment that supports a smart city system.

Concrete steps are being taken through potential collaboration with the Association of Telecommunications Network Operators (Apjatel), particularly in organizing optical cables to be buried underground in an integrated manner.

The collaboration was discussed in a meeting between the Deputy Regent of Sumedang, M. Fajar Aldila, and Regional Secretary Tuti Ruswati with Apjatel West Java Regional Coordinator, chaired by Dudi Sudrajat Abdurachim, on Friday (5/23/2025) in the Deputy Regent's office.

“Underground cables are not just about city aesthetics, but also support the acceleration of digital transformation towards a smart city, minimize fire risks, and create a safe and orderly urban space,” explained Dudi.

He emphasized that the systematic arrangement of fiber optic cables is one of the key elements in supporting a more organized and people-friendly city landscape.

In addition to disrupting the city's visual appearance, cables hanging haphazardly are also considered a safety hazard.

Meanwhile, Deputy Regent Fajar highlighted the urgency of organizing the cable network in the Jatinangor area.

This area is considered strategic due to its multicultural character and high population density.

He said that underground cable management in this area could serve as a model for the integration of technology-based surveillance systems.

“Young people there are active on social media. If our city planning is neat and modern, it will certainly have a positive impact. We are currently developing a smart pole system with poles containing drones, CCTV, and operators to monitor security, because Jatinangor is now quite vulnerable,” he said.

Fajar also emphasized the importance of underground cable integration in supporting community activities, especially in terms of digital transactions, which have now become an essential need.

However, he reminded the importance of anticipatory measures given Sumedang's unstable geological conditions.

“There must be exploratory and topographical studies to ensure that cable installation does not cause problems in the future. Because the costs will be borne by the regional budget if a disaster or damage occurs,” he emphasized.

He hopes that the discussion on cooperation between the Sumedang Regency Government and Apjatel can be immediately followed up with implementation.

“If the budget is available and the program is on target, why not implement it immediately?” he concluded.

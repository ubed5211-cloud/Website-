Sumedang's Smart City Initiatives

Sumedang is developing smart city initiatives with projects like smart poles and panic buttons to improve public services.

Sumedang, Gizmology â€“ Smart city initiatives continue to be promoted as a city development concept that utilizes information and communication technology (ICT) to improve the quality of life of citizens, the efficiency of public services, and resource management. One of the areas currently developing Smart City is Sumedang Regency, which has long been synonymous with tofu.

Recently, the Sumedang Regency Government signed two agreements with PT Alita Praya Mitra (Alita) regarding the trial installation of Smart Pole and the development of Smart City in Sumedang Regency. Sumedang Regent Dony Ahmad Munir, after signing the agreement, said that Alita has contributed and worked quite well with the Sumedang Regency Government.

"The other day we were given smart wearable 'Sinurmi' to detect blood pressure, heart rate, oxygen saturation, and others for pregnant women to prevent stunting," said Dony (15/3).

Sumedang Development into Smart City

Dony added, to present a smart city, his party collaborated with Alita and PT Edotco Infrastruktur Indonesia. Currently, the collaboration is present with innovation for Sumedang Regency, namely smart pole assistance in the form of poles equipped with various sensors and devices to support various functions and can be used for the benefit of the government and the community.

"This smart pole is a smart pole that has several facilities including CCTV, network reinforcement, environmental sensors, public information and advertising facilities, then for charging electric vehicles, and the plan is that there will also be drones," he said.

The Regent said that the presence of smart poles is one of the efforts to improve services to the community and is planned to be installed in May 2025. "On this pole, there is a tool to assess the quality of the air we breathe, and there is a panic button that will be connected to the Regional Government Command Center and to the 112 service," he said.

Meanwhile, drones will be used to monitor an environment to maintain a security system. "Later, these drones will monitor the environment, especially to maintain a smart environmental security system," he said.

Meanwhile, Teguh Prasetya as President Director of PT. Alita Praya Mitra said that his party was proud to be able to continue to support the Sumedang Regional Government. "This is a source of pride for us to be able to support the Sumedang Regional Government to continue to move forward, provide the best service to the community, increase community productivity and also increase security for the community," he explained.

Teguh also conveyed his initiative in building the smart pole, namely in order to improve the quality of services, be it telecommunications services, information, and more advanced public services. "So with this, we hope that the productivity of the Sumedang community will also increase," he concluded.
